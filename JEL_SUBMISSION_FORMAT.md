## Submission Requirements

### Word Count
- **Requirement**: 6,000-10,000 words
- **Current**: 9,000+ ✓
- **Status**: MEETS REQUIREMENT (slightly long; may need trimming)

### Format
- **Style**: Comprehensive survey/review articles
- **Focus**: Synthesis + critical assessment + research agenda
- **Structure**: Context → Methods → Findings → Critique → Future Work
- **Abstract**: 100-150 words (yours: 160 words) - SLIGHTLY LONG
- **Keywords**: 5-8 terms (yours: 8) ✓

### Key Differences from JES
1. **Tone**: More critical and evaluative (not just synthesis)
2. **Theory**: Heavier emphasis on theoretical contributions
3. **Critique**: Explicit discussion of what's missing/wrong in literature
4. **Novelty**: Must identify NEW gaps or NOVEL frameworks
5. **Word Limit**: Stricter (6,000-10,000 vs JES's 8,000-12,000)

### Formatting Specifications
1. **Title**: Max 12 words, include "Survey" or "Review"
2. **Headings**: Major sections only (no excessive subsections)
3. **References**: Chicago style author-date (similar to APA)
4. **Tables**: Max 3-4 in main text (yours: 6, REDUCE to 4)
5. **Figures**: Max 2-3 (yours: 3) ✓
6. **Footnotes**: Use substantively, not just citations (max 15)
7. **Equations**: Numbered only if essential
8. **Paragraphs**: Dense, paragraph-level organization

### Reference Style for JEL
- Inline: (Author Year) or Author (Year)
- List: Last name, First name. Year. "Title of article." *Journal Name*, Vol(Issue):pp–pp.
- Example: Tetlock, Paul C. 2007. "Giving content to investor sentiment." *Journal of Finance*, 62(3):1139–68.

### Special Requirements for JEL
1. ✓ Abstract highlighting NEW insights
2. ✗ JEL Codes (JEL doesn't use these; uses classification instead)
3. ✓ Keywords (4-6)
4. ✓ Explicit discussion of what's missing in literature
5. ✓ Theoretical contribution or framework
6. ✓ Critical assessment of methods/findings
7. ✓ Implications for future research
