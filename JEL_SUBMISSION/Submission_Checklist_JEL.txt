IMPORTANT: JEL IS HIGHLY SELECTIVE (5-10% acceptance)
Emphasis on novelty, critical insights, and research gaps

MANUSCRIPT PREPARATION
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
□ Apply editing guide: Trim to 8,200 words
□ Abstract: 100-120 words (down from 160)
□ Title: Include "Critical Assessment" or "Unresolved Questions"
□ Keywords: 4-6 terms (not 8)
□ Word count: 8,200 exactly (no more than 8,500)
□ Tables: 4 main + 2 appendix (reduce from 6)
□ Figures: 3 (keep all)
□ Line spacing: 1.5
□ Margins: 1 inch all sides
□ Font: 12pt Times New Roman (JEL prefers larger)

CRITICAL: JEL EMPHASIS
─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
✓ UNRESOLVED QUESTIONS (Major focus):
  □ Causality ambiguity clearly stated
  □ Information vs. noise debate explained
  □ Geographic/linguistic gaps quantified
  □ Why these gaps matter (implications)

✓ CRITICAL ASSESSMENT (Not just synthesis):
  □ Real-time protocol violations identified (35%)
  □ Publication bias discussed
  □ Validation gaps articulated
  □ Theoretical weaknesses highlighted

✓ RESEARCH AGENDA (Novel contribution):
  □ Specific next steps proposed
  □ Barriers identified
  □ Feasibility discussed
  □ Impact estimated

Different from other journals:
✓ Stricter word limits (6,000-10,000)
✓ Higher selectivity (5-10% acceptance)
