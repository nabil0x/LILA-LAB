        EDITING GUIDE: PREPARING JEL VERSION (9,000 → 8,200 words)
═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

JEL has stricter word limits than JES. This guide helps trim manuscript 
while maintaining quality and rigor.

WORD COUNT BUDGET: 800 words to cut

Strategy: Remove examples, consolidate sections, maintain rigor

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SECTION 1: ABSTRACT (CUT 20 words)
Current: 160 words
Target: 120-140 words
Strategy: Remove specific examples, keep core tensions

Original:
"We conduct a comprehensive meta-analysis establishing that sentiment-based 
models improve forecast accuracy by approximately 20% (median RMSE reduction), 
with substantial variation by domain: 18% for GDP, 22% for inflation, 28% 
for volatility."

Trimmed:
"Meta-analysis of 66 studies establishes 20% median RMSE improvement 
(range 5–42%), with variation by domain. However, critical unresolved 
questions persist..."

Savings: ~25 words

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SECTION 2: INTRODUCTION (CUT 100-150 words)
Current: ~800 words
Target: ~700 words
Strategy: Cut redundant phrases, tighten transitions

Examples to cut:
- "from Tetlock (2007)'s analysis of Wall Street Journal sentiment 
  predicting stock returns, to recent LLM-based decomposition of 
  macroeconomic narratives (Kwon et al., 2025)"
  → Replace with: "spanning dictionary-based approaches (Tetlock 2007) 
     to emerging LLM methods (Kwon et al. 2025)"
  
  Savings: 20 words

- Redundant phrase: "Despite 18 years of research and methodological 
  sophistication, critical gaps remain. Geographic bias is stark..."
  → Tighten to: "Despite 18 years of research, critical gaps remain: 
     geographic bias is stark..."
     
  Savings: 10 words

Total intro cuts: ~120 words

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SECTION 3: METHODOLOGICAL EVOLUTION (CUT 100-150 words)
Current: ~1,000 words
Target: ~850-900 words
Strategy: Remove detailed examples, keep essentials

Cut these details:
- Dictionary subsection example: "Recognition that general dictionaries 
  misclassify financial language ("liability" marked negative) motivated..."
  → Simply state: "Domain-specific alternatives emerged (Loughran-McDonald 2011)"
  
  Savings: 30 words

- ML subsection: Detailed description of each algorithm
  → Consolidate into 2-3 sentences
  
  Savings: 50 words

- BERT/LLM descriptions: Too technical for JEL
  → Condense to: "Pre-trained transformers (BERT, RoBERTa) achieve 85-92% 
     accuracy; LLMs enable zero-shot classification"
     
  Savings: 40 words

Total section cuts: ~120 words

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SECTION 4: RESULTS SUBSECTIONS (CUT 150-200 words)
Current: Multiple detailed subsections
Strategy: Consolidate tables, remove redundant discussions

- Results 4.2: Remove detailed example papers
  Keep: "36 papers (55%) use dictionaries; 4 (6%) use BERT"
  Cut: Individual examples like "Tetlock (2007) pioneered..."
  
  Savings: 60 words

- Results 4.3: Consolidate performance discussion
  Keep: "Median 20% improvement, 15-25% economically meaningful"
  Cut: Repeated examples: "GDP forecasting (22 papers) shows median RMSE..."
  
  Savings: 80 words

Total cuts: ~150 words

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

SECTION 5: GEOGRAPHIC COVERAGE (CUT 100 words)
Current: ~600 words
Target: ~500 words
Strategy: Keep gap analysis, cut descriptive details

- Current paragraph: "The geographic distribution of research shows stark 
  concentration: North America hosts 37 papers (56%), Europe 13 (20%), 
  East/Southeast Asia 9 (14%), and South Asia 4 (6%). Zero coverage in 
  Africa, Latin America, and the Middle East represents a profound research 
  gap relative to economic significance."

- Trimmed: "Geographic concentration: US 56%, Europe 26%, Asia 14%, South Asia 4%, 
  Africa/Latin America 0%. Zero indices exist for 4 billion people."
  
  Savings: 40 words

- Remove redundant implications discussion
  Savings: 60 words

Total cuts: ~100 words

═══════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════════

Option A: Move two minor tables to appendix
  Current: 6 tables in main text
  New: 4 tables main + 2 appendix
  Savings: ~80-100 words (caption, cross-references)

Option B: Consolidate validation discussion
  Savings: ~50 words
