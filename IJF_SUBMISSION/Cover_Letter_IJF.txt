Editor, International Journal of Forecasting

Dear Editor,

We submit for review: "Validating Sentiment-Based Economic Forecasts: A
Meta-Analysis of 66 Studies and a Best-Practices Framework"

This manuscript provides forecasters with the first comprehensive evidence
base on how much sentiment-based models improve economic forecasts—and
how to validate them rigorously. It directly addresses IJF's core mission
of advancing forecasting methodology.

KEY CONTRIBUTIONS FOR FORECASTERS:

1. META-ANALYTIC PERFORMANCE ESTIMATES:
   ╔══════════════════════════════════════════════════════╗
   ║ Median RMSE improvement: 20% (range 5–42%)          ║
   ║ GDP: 18% | Inflation: 22% | Volatility: 28%         ║
   ║ Dictionary: 15% | ML: 22% | BERT: 24% | LLM: 28%   ║
   ╚══════════════════════════════════════════════════════╝
   All estimates include publication bias sensitivity analysis.
   These provide forecasters quantitative benchmarks for sizing
   expected improvements across methods and domains.

2. VALIDATION PRACTICE AUDIT:
   • 83% use out-of-sample testing (good baseline)
   • 71% use Diebold-Mariano tests (rigorous)
   • 33% follow real-time data protocols (→ CRITICAL GAP)
   • 18% use Model Confidence Sets (advanced)
   
   We estimate ~35% of published papers violate real-time discipline,
   inflating reported improvements by 1-3%. This is the first
   quantification of this gap in the sentiment forecasting literature.

3. BEST-PRACTICES VALIDATION FRAMEWORK:
   Tier 1 (Essential): OOS + rolling window + DM test + real-time
   Tier 2 (Recommended): + sub-sample robustness + alternative measures
   Tier 3 (Advanced): + MCS/SPA + bootstrap confidence intervals
   
   This tiered system helps forecasters calibrate validation rigor
   to their deployment context.

4. PRACTITIONER DECISION FRAMEWORK:
   A concrete decision guide mapping forecast horizon, data availability,
   budget, and interpretability requirements to recommended methods:
   • Decision flow: horizon → data → budget → interpretability
   • Method × Use-case matrix (real-time, batch, high-freq, policy)
   • Performance trade-offs table (accuracy, speed, cost, transparency)
   • 7 common practitioner pitfalls with mitigations

5. METHOD COMPARISON FOR DEPLOYMENT:
   • When to use dictionary vs. ML vs. BERT vs. LLM
   • Accuracy vs. interpretability vs. cost trade-offs quantified
   • Comparison with traditional indicators (PMI, yield spreads, ARIMA)

6. DEPLOYMENT CONSIDERATIONS:
   56% US focus, 0% Africa/Latin America—emerging markets have the
   most to gain from sentiment forecasting (sparse macro data) but
   zero evidence to guide method selection.

ORIGINALITY & SIGNIFICANCE:

This is the first systematic review of sentiment-based economic forecasting
framed as a FORECASTING METHODOLOGY paper with practitioner deployment focus.
Previous surveys (Kearney & Liu 2014, Gentzkow et al. 2019) catalogued
methods; this paper provides validated decision guidance for forecasters.

The practitioner decision framework—mapping forecast characteristics to
optimal methods with quantified trade-offs—is entirely novel to this
literature and directly serves IJF's practitioner readership.
