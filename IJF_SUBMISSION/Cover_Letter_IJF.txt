Dear Editor,

We submit for review: "Sentiment-Based Economic Forecasting: A Systematic 
Review of Validation Practices, Performance Metrics, and Best Practices 
for Forecasters (2007–2025)"

This manuscript synthesizes 66 empirical studies on sentiment-based 
economic forecasting, with specific focus on validation practices and 
practical deployment guidance for forecasters—the core mission of IJF.

KEY CONTRIBUTIONS FOR PRACTITIONERS:

1. VALIDATION AUDIT: Quantifies validation practice adoption:
   • 83% use out-of-sample testing (good baseline)
   • 71% use Diebold-Mariano statistical tests (rigorous)
   • 33% follow real-time data protocols (CRITICAL GAP)
   • Only 18% use Model Confidence Sets (advanced)
   
   This gap is actionable: we identify that ~35% of papers violate 
   real-time discipline, likely inflating reported improvements by 1-3%.

2. BEST-PRACTICES FRAMEWORK: Novel three-tier validation system:
   • Tier 1 (Essential): OOS + rolling window + DM test + real-time
   • Tier 2 (Recommended): + sub-sample robustness + alternative measures
   • Tier 3 (Advanced): + MCS/SPA + bootstrap confidence intervals
   
   This tiered approach helps forecasters choose appropriate rigor 
   for their deployment context.

3. PERFORMANCE SYNTHESIS: Meta-analysis of 66 studies:
   • Median RMSE improvement: 20% (range 5–42%)
   • Variation by domain: 18% GDP, 22% inflation, 28% volatility
   • Variation by method: Dictionary (15%), ML (22%), BERT (24%), LLM (28%)
   • All estimates include publication bias sensitivity analysis
   
   This provides quantitative evidence for practitioners sizing 
   expected improvements.

4. DECISION TREE FOR PRACTITIONERS: Actionable guidance:
   • "What method should I use?" → Decision tree by horizon/budget/latency
   • Performance trade-offs matrix (accuracy vs. speed vs. cost vs. transparency)
   • Real-time protocol checklist (20-point audit tool)
   • Common pitfalls to avoid (7 specific errors identified in literature)

5. EMERGING MARKET APPLICABILITY: 
   • Analysis of geographic coverage shows 56% US focus, 0% Africa/Latin America
   • For emerging markets with sparse real-time macro data, sentiment 
     indices offer practical alternative
   • Example: Bangladesh Bank could deploy BanglaBERT for 24-hour 
     inflation sentiment nowcasts

ORIGINALITY & SIGNIFICANCE:

This is the first systematic review of sentiment-based economic forecasting 
with explicit focus on validation practices and practitioner deployment. 
Previous surveys (Kearney & Liu 2014, Gentzkow et al. 2019) emphasized 
methodology; this review uniquely emphasizes validation rigor and real-world 
deployment constraints.

The identification of the "real-time protocol gap" (only 33% compliance) 
is significant: it implies 1-3% inflation of reported improvements, 
qualitatively changing the evidence base for practitioners.
