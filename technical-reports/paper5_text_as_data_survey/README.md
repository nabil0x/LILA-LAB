# Paper 5: Text as Data in Social Science — A Survey

**Full Title:** Text as Data in Social Science: A Survey with Applications to Low-Resource Languages

**Status:** Planned (Target: October 2026)

---

## Description

This paper provides a comprehensive survey of text-as-data methods in the social sciences, with a focus on their application to low-resource language settings. It synthesizes the methodological landscape — from dictionary-based approaches to modern large language model pipelines — and draws on empirical findings from the first four papers in the LILA Lab series to illustrate key concepts and trade-offs.

### Scope

The survey will cover:

- **Theoretical foundations** — why text is a meaningful source of social science measurement (representation, framing, agenda-setting theories)
- **Methodological approaches** — dictionary methods, supervised learning, topic models, word embeddings, transformer-based classification, LLM annotation
- **Validation strategies** — internal (human annotation agreement, classifier accuracy) and external (correlation with established indicators) validation
- **Low-resource challenges** — data scarcity, dialect variation, domain adaptation, model bias, and evaluation in the absence of gold-standard benchmarks
- **Domain applications** — economics, political science, sociology, public health, and climate communication
- **The BENI pipeline** — in-depth case study of how the XENI framework addresses low-resource challenges in practice
- **Future directions** — cross-language comparisons, multi-modal narratives, real-time monitoring, and the role of LLMs as measurement instruments

### Relationship to Papers 1–4

This survey paper serves as a synthesis of the LILA Lab research program:

- **Paper 1** (Statistical Economics of Narrative) provides the theoretical grounding for measuring narratives quantitatively.
- **Paper 2** (Systematic Review) maps the existing literature that this survey organizes and extends.
- **Paper 3** (Building BENI Pipeline) provides the detailed methodological case study.
- **Paper 4** (Nowcasting Inflation) demonstrates the applied value proposition that motivates broader adoption.

By drawing these threads together, the survey aims to lower the barrier for social scientists in low-resource settings who want to incorporate text-as-data methods into their research.
