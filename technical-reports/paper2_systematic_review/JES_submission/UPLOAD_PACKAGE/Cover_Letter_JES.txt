Dear Editor,

I am pleased to submit my manuscript, "Economic Narrative Indices and
Media-Based Sentiment Measures: A Systematic Review of Methodologies,
Applications, and Research Gaps (2007--2025)," for consideration as a Survey
Article in the Journal of Economic Surveys.

The manuscript reviews 46 empirical studies and 20 theoretical papers on the
use of sentiment and narrative measures in economic forecasting. It is closest
to Algaba et al. (2020), also published in the Journal of Economic Surveys, but
it takes a narrower forecasting-oriented perspective. The review asks how much
predictive value sentiment measures add out of sample, how reported gains vary
with benchmark strength, what validation practices are used, and where the
evidence base remains geographically or linguistically thin.

This systematic review fully follows the Journal of Economic Surveys' reporting
guidelines for systematic reviews in economics (Havranek et al., 2020).
The search strategy — including the databases queried (Scopus, Web of Science,
EconLit, arXiv), the full search-term string, and the date range (2007–2025) —
is documented in the manuscript's methodology section and the supplementary
coding matrix. The submission includes a PRISMA flow diagram, coded literature
matrix, and structured quality assessment. It also cites Irsova et al. (2024)
as a methodological reference for practitioners conducting meta-analyses in
this domain. The supplementary materials provide a fully coded literature
matrix with audit trail,
quality-assessment scores for all 66 papers, and raw data for the quantitative
synthesis.

The manuscript makes four contributions. First, it provides a quantitative
synthesis of reported forecast improvements, distinguishing results against
weak autoregressive baselines from results against stronger benchmarks such as
ARIMA models, professional forecasts, and published nowcasts. Second, it
organizes the methods used in the literature, from dictionary-based measures to
machine learning, transformer models, and early large-language-model
applications. Third, it audits validation practice, including out-of-sample
testing, real-time data discipline, Diebold-Mariano testing, Model Confidence
Sets, and reporting of annotation reliability. Fourth, it documents the
geographic and linguistic concentration of the literature and uses the Bangla
Economic Narrative Index as an illustrative roadmap for future work in
underrepresented language settings.

The submission includes a clean supplementary coding matrix and quality
assessment file. These files provide one row per reviewed paper, coded columns
for method, validation, benchmark type, region, language, and standardized
forecast-performance entries where comparable information is available.

The manuscript is approximately 10,200 words, contains three main-text tables and
four figures, and is anonymized for blind review. The title page provides author
and contact information separately. The manuscript is original, has not been
published previously, and is not under consideration elsewhere. I declare no
competing financial interests.

Thank you for considering the manuscript.

Sincerely,
