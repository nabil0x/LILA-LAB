Please find attached my manuscript entitled "Economic Narrative
Indices and Media-Based Sentiment Measures: A Systematic Review
of Methodologies, Applications, and Research Gaps (2007–2025)" for
consideration for publication in The Jahangirnagar Economic Review,
Volume 37 (June 2026).

This systematic review synthesizes 66 studies on sentiment-based
economic forecasting. The paper is motivated by a simple asymmetry:
text-based economic indicators are increasingly useful where data
systems are already strong, but they remain scarce where real-time
signals may matter most. It therefore treats the literature not only as
a set of methods, but also as a map of what the field has chosen to
see, and what it has so far left outside the frame.

The manuscript contributes:

  • A quantitative synthesis documenting a pooled median 20% RMSE
    reduction from sentiment-enhanced forecasting models, with a more
    conservative 10–15% value-added range after accounting for
    publication bias and baseline strength
  • A structured methodological taxonomy spanning dictionary-based,
    ML, transformer, and LLM approaches
  • A tiered validation framework standardizing best practices
  • Explicit documentation of geographic and linguistic research gaps:
    56% of studies focus on the US, 84% on English text, and zero
    sentiment indices exist for Africa, Latin America, or Bangla-
    speaking regions (265 million speakers)
  • A concrete expansion proposal: the Bangla Economic Narrative
    Index (BENI), a native-language economic sentiment index for
    Bangla-speaking markets

I believe the paper fits JER's readership because it connects a fast-
moving methodological literature with an economic problem that is
locally relevant: how researchers and policy institutions in countries
such as Bangladesh can build their own high-frequency narrative
measures rather than importing tools calibrated elsewhere.
